Why judges use DebateClock for Policy

Policy debate uses per-team prep rather than per-debater. Each team has one 8-minute pool shared between both partners across the full round. That's hard to track manually when speeches run 64 minutes of speaking plus CX.

DebateClock loads all 12 speeches with correct CX positions flagged, and the prep pool auto-pauses whenever a speech starts. Tap Start Prep — Aff or Start Prep — Neg and either partner's prep draws from the same pool.

How to run a Policy round

Set up takes under a minute:

  • Judge opens debateclock.org/app/ on their phone. A room code appears in the top-right.
  • Tap COPY to send the debater link, or have them scan the QR code on screen.
  • Select Policy Debate from the format picker. Tap Start to begin the 1AC.
  • When a team requests prep, tap Start Prep — Aff or Start Prep — Neg. Either partner can draw from the 8-minute team pool. The active speech auto-pauses.
  • Tap the next speech in the right-hand list to advance. Or use keyboard: → for next, space for start/pause, A or N for prep.

Frequently asked questions

What are the speech times in Policy debate?
Standard NSDA Policy uses 4 constructives (1AC, 1NC, 2AC, 2NC) at 8 minutes each, 4 cross-examinations at 3 minutes each, and 4 rebuttals (1NR, 1AR, 2NR, 2AR) at 5 minutes each. Each team has a shared 8-minute prep pool.
How does team prep work?
Each team has one 8-minute pool. Either the 1st or 2nd speaker can request prep time. When one partner uses the pool, it draws down for the whole team. It's not per-debater like in Lincoln-Douglas.
Does this support flex-prep?
Yes. Flex-prep formats just have a larger prep pool — adjust with the +30s button during the round, or use the reset to start over. DebateClock doesn't lock you into a single timing variant.
Can I use this at NDT/CEDA tournaments?
Yes. DebateClock is format-agnostic — it doesn't communicate with any tournament management system. It's just a timer. Free to use at any level of competition.
Does the judge need to install anything?
No. Open debateclock.org/app/ in any browser. Room code appears automatically. The debater gets the same link via copy or QR.
