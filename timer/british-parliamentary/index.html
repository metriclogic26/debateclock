Two BP formats, one timer

British Parliamentary splits into two common variants. Both use the same 4-team structure but differ on speech length and the exact POI window boundaries. DebateClock has both preloaded.

BP UNIVERSITY

WUDC / University Circuit

7-minute speeches across all 8 positions. POI window: minutes 1:00 through 6:00. First and last minute protected. Standard at WUDC and most national university competitions.

BP HIGH SCHOOL

High School Circuit

5-minute speeches. POI window: 0:30 through 4:30 — 30 seconds protected at both start and end. This variant is explicitly missing from most mobile debate timers.

Why judges use DebateClock for BP

BP judging requires tracking which of the 4 teams is speaking and whether the POI window is open — at the same time as flowing four simultaneous cases and tracking comparative rankings. The POI window cue on the debater display removes one thing the judge would otherwise have to manage manually.

Frequently asked questions

What are the speech times in British Parliamentary?
University BP (WUDC) uses 7-minute speeches. High school BP uses 5-minute speeches. Both formats have 8 speeches across 4 teams in the same order.
When is the POI window open in BP?
University BP: the window opens at 1:00 and closes at 6:00, leaving one minute protected at each end. High school BP: the window opens at 0:30 and closes at 4:30, leaving 30 seconds protected at each end.
Is there prep time in BP?
No. British Parliamentary has no in-round prep pool. Teams prepare before the round starts; there is no prep between speeches.
Which BP format should I use for WUDC?
Select "British Parliamentary (Uni / WUDC)" from the format picker. It uses 7-minute speeches and a POI window from 1:00 to 6:00, matching the standard used at WUDC and most university competitions.
Does the timer work for Canadian Parliamentary (CUSID)?
Canadian Parliamentary is very close to BP University — 7-minute speeches, same POI window structure. The BP University preset covers most CUSID rounds. Asian Parliamentary (also 7-min with similar POI rules) is on the roadmap.
