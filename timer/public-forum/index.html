Why judges use DebateClock for Public Forum

Public Forum has the tightest prep budget in competitive debate — 3 minutes per team for the entire round. Teams use it strategically before summaries and final focuses, which means tracking it accurately matters.

How to run a Public Forum round

Set up takes under a minute:

  • Judge opens debateclock.org/app/ on their phone. A room code appears in the top-right.
  • Tap COPY to send the debater link, or have them scan the QR code on screen.
  • Select Public Forum from the format picker. Tap Start to begin the first constructive.
  • When a team requests prep, tap Start Prep — Aff (Pro) or Start Prep — Neg (Con). The active speech auto-pauses.
  • Tap the next speech in the right-hand list to advance. Or use keyboard: → for next, space for start/pause, A or N for prep.

Frequently asked questions

What are the speech times in Public Forum?
NSDA Public Forum 2025-26 uses: two 4-minute constructives per team, 3-minute crossfires after each, two 3-minute summaries, a 3-minute grand crossfire, and two 2-minute final focuses. Each team has a 3-minute prep pool.
How does prep work in PF?
Each team has a 3-minute pool for the full round. Teams typically reserve most of it for before summaries and final focuses, where it matters most. Either teammate can request prep.
What is grand crossfire?
Grand crossfire is a 3-minute crossfire between all four debaters at once, right before final focus. All four speakers are standing and questioning each other simultaneously.
Does the timer work with the 4-3-2 variant?
Yes. Some leagues use shorter speeches. DebateClock defaults to NSDA 2025-26 timings, but the +30s and -30s buttons let the judge adjust on the fly.
Is there a signup or login?
No. Open debateclock.org/app/, select Public Forum, and run the round. No account, no email, no tracking.
